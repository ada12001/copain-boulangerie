Four Ingredients. A Lifetime of Craft.

Bread as It Was Meant to Be

Every loaf begins with flour, water, salt, and a starter that has been alive for nearly four decades. Our process is unhurried — long fermentation, careful shaping, and a bake that honours the grain. Nothing is added that does not belong.

Country Sourdough

Pain de Campagne

The foundational Copain loaf. Slow-fermented and naturally leavened, it bakes into a deeply caramelized crust with an open, tender crumb and the kind of quiet acidity that makes butter, cheese, and olive oil feel complete rather than necessary.

Baguette Tradition

La Baguette

Thin crust, creamy center, and a clean wheat finish. Each baguette is shaped by hand, proofed with patience, and baked with steam for that unmistakable snap at first break. It is a classic because the method still works exactly as it should.

Seeded Levain

Sesame & Poppy Crust

A darker, more savory loaf layered with toasted sesame and poppy seed. The crust brings fragrance and crackle; the interior stays moist, supple, and deeply flavorful. It is the bread we love beside cured fish, strong cheeses, roasted vegetables, and long lunches.

Hearth Miche

Grand Pain

Built for the center of the table, this large-format loaf is baked hard for a bold crust and an interior that stays fresh beautifully over time. Cut thick, toasted the next day, or served with supper service, it is a bread made for kitchens that want generosity as much as flavor.
